## 2. Simple points algorithm

You can explain this to engineers as configuration rather than hard-coded values.

### A. Base points per mission

Assign each mission a difficulty:

- **Easy** (short, simple): 10 points  
- **Medium** (needs interaction/travel): 15 points  
- **Hard** (office hours, workshops, off-campus): 20 points  

Examples:
- Easy: Harvard Yard Chill Spot, Self-Care Moment  
- Medium: Dining Hall Debut, Ask an American, Transportation Trial  
- Hard: Office Hours Explorer, Off-campus Explorer, Workshop Bonus

### B. Bonus points

Per submission:

1. **Group size bonus**
   - 1 person: +0  
   - 2–3 people in the photo: +3  
   - 4–6 people: +5  
   - Cap at 6 to prevent huge staged crowds skewing the game.

2. **Nationality diversity bonus**
   - If the mission allows multiple participants to tag their country:
     - 1–2 countries represented: +0  
     - 3+ countries: +3 bonus

3. **Photo feed sharing bonus**
   - If user checks “Share this photo to the public feed”: +2 points
   - This encourages content but doesn’t overpower mission points.

4. **Limited-time “event” bonus**
   - For missions flagged as time-limited and completed within their active window: +5 bonus

5. **Badge/achievement bonuses** (auto-calculated)
   - Resource Master badge: +20 once  
   - Global Connector badge: +20 once  
   - Campus Explorer badge (e.g., complete 5 location missions): +20 once

### C. Daily/weekly caps & anti-gaming

- **Per mission**:  
  - Each mission can be fully completed only **once per student** (badges and weekly event missions can repeat but with decreasing returns if you want).
- **Daily cap** (optional):  
  - First 5 completed missions per day earn full points; any additional missions that day earn 50% of their normal base points.
- **Location checks**:
  - Require GPS to be inside the mission’s geofence.
- **QR/NFC checks**:
  - For some key missions, you only get completion if QR/NFC is scanned (so they must truly go inside offices/events).

### D. Individual and group scoring

- **Individual score**: Sum of all their mission points + bonuses + badges.
- **Group score** (choose one model):
  1. **House-based/team-based**:
     - Each student belongs to exactly one team (e.g., by House/Yard or self-chosen team).
     - Team score = sum of the **top N** members’ scores (e.g., top 10 in that team) to avoid big teams always winning.
  2. **Average score model**:
     - Team score = average of member scores; helps smaller teams compete.
